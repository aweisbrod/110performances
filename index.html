Top five places I've performed with

THE OHIO UNIVERSITY MARCHING 110

The Marching 110 performs their pre-game show in 2012 at Peden Stadium in Athens, Ohio. Photo by Nick Bolin.

By Amanda Weisbrod

During my time in the Ohio University Marching 110, I've been a part of some pretty incredible performances — I played alto saxophone in front of the Eiffel Tower, at a bowl game in the Bahamas, on the Today Show, during halftime at NFL games, and in the Macy's Thanksgiving Parade, just to name a few of the more notable ones.

No matter where we marched, grooved and danced, I always had an incredible time, but some performances stick out more than others. Check out this list of my top five favorite places I've performed with the Marching 110 from my first season in 2014 to my last season in 2017.

01 PEDEN STADIUM

Band members parch with pregame flags bearing the logo of the Marching 110's 50th anniversary at the 2017 homecoming football game. Photo by Nick Bolin.

Date: 2014–2017
Location: Athens, Ohio
My favorite performances were almost all at home in Peden Stadium because our student section has more energy and love for OU's marching band than any other university I've seen.

One halftime show in particular sticks out — in 2015, the Bobcats were facing off against Marshall University in the Battle for the Bell, a yearly rivalry game. The Marching 110 played a Bruno Mars show, featuring top hit "Uptown Funk," and the student section went absolutely nuts.

02 EIFFEL TOWER

The Marching 110 performs Long Train Running, one of its signature songs, in front of the Eiffel Tower. Photo by Nick Bolin.

Date: May 2016
Location: Paris, France
Now this is a trip I'll never forget. In the summer after my freshman year at Ohio University, the 110 traveled across seas to Paris. We not only danced in front of the Eiffel Tower, but also paraded through Disneyland Paris and performed a solemn yet moving set of patriotic songs at the WWII American Cemetary in Normandy.

03 BAHAMAS BOWL

The upperclassmen of the 2017 Marching 110 pose for a group photo after a pep rally performance in the Bahamas. Photo by Nick Bolin.

Date: December 2017
Location: New Providence, Bahamas
Every year we prayed the Bobcats would get scheduled for the MAC bowl game in the Bahamas, and my senior year, our prayers were answered.

This was an expensive trip to cover, so only the upperclassmen of the marching band were able to go. We spent three days hanging out on the beach, going down water slides and gambling in a casino. It was an amazing end to my time in the Marching 110.

04 MACY'S PARADE

The Marching 110 marches in the 2017 Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade. Photo by Nick Bolin.

Date: November 2017
Location: New York, New York
Also in my senior year, we rose a bus all the way from Athens to New York City (a long and grueling trip complete with bus breakdowns and horrendous traffic) to march in the Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade — the ultimate dream of all marching bands. I have never been so excited for a parade in my life.

While we were in the city, I also got to see the Book of Mormon on Broadway and explore Times Square. It was my very first time in the Big Apple, so it was overall an amazing experience.

05 UNIV. OF TENNESSEE

The Marching 110 performs a country-music-themed halftime show during an away game at the University of Tennessee. Photo by Nick Bolin.

Date: September 2016
Location: Knoxville, Tennessee
Last on my top five is the football game between the Ohio University Bobcats and the University of Tennessee Volunteers. It was insanely hot in the stands, but when we took the field for halftime, everyone gave it their all.

We performed a country music show because we were in the south, including square dancing and a lasso move during our dance section. The crowd, who were mostly made up of the opposing team's fans, went absolutely wild for it.
